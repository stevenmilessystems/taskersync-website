Pastor Productivity Tools - What Actually Moves the Needle

By TaskerSync · March 2026 · 7 min read

Most productivity advice is written for office workers or entrepreneurs. Pastors have a different problem - the work is relational, the hours are irregular, and the to-do list never ends.

1. Google Tasks + TaskerSync - Your Task List, Automated

Google Tasks is already on your phone, watch, and Gmail sidebar. TaskerSync plugs into it and actions your tasks automatically - research, drafts, answers - written back into your notes in seconds. It's not a new app. It's making the app you already use dramatically more powerful.

2. Google Calendar - For Protecting Your Time

Block time for sermon prep, admin, and family - not just meetings. Calendar blocking is the single most effective pastoral time management habit. If it's not blocked, someone will fill it.

3. Elvanto / Planning Center - Church Management

For rosters, volunteer coordination, and service planning. Elvanto is popular in Australian churches; Planning Center is dominant in the US. Both save significant admin time once set up.

4. Loom - For Async Communication

Record a 2-minute video instead of writing a 10-minute email. Great for communicating vision, giving feedback, or explaining something to a volunteer without a full meeting.

5. Notion - For Sermon Series and Long-Term Planning

Build a database of sermon series, teaching notes, and resources. Notion's linked databases let you connect sermons to passages, themes, and series - useful for avoiding repeated content.

The Honest Answer

No tool replaces good habits. But the right tools reduce the friction that stops good habits from forming. Start with your task list and make it smarter first.
